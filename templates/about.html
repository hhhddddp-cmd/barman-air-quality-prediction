About This Project

Understanding Air Quality Prediction with Machine Learning

What is This Project?

The India Air Quality Prediction system is an advanced machine learning application designed to forecast PM2.5 (Particulate Matter 2.5) levels based on various environmental pollutants. This project uses historical air quality data from across India to train a Random Forest regression model.

PM2.5 refers to tiny particles in the air that are 2.5 microns or less in diameter. These particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream, posing serious health risks.

Health Impact

PM2.5 monitoring helps protect public health

Air Quality Index (AQI) Categories

Good (0-30)

Air quality is satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

Satisfactory (31-60)

Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people.

Moderate (61-90)

Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects.

Poor (91-120)

The risk of health effects is increased for everyone.

Very Poor (121-250)

Health warning: everyone is more likely to be affected.

Severe (250+)

Health alert: Everyone may experience serious health effects.
